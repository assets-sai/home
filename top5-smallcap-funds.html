Jargon Buster — What These Metrics Mean

Understanding every parameter in the analysis above
MetricWhat It Tells YouIdeal Value
CAGRCompounded Annual Growth Rate — smoothed annual return over a periodHigher = Better
Expense RatioAnnual fee charged by the fund house; directly reduces your returnsLower = Better
Sharpe RatioReturn earned per unit of risk taken; measures efficiency of returns> 1.0 is Good
AlphaExcess return over benchmark after adjusting for risk; shows manager skillPositive = Better
BetaSensitivity to market moves; Beta < 1 = less volatile than market< 1.0 = Less Risky
Std DeviationMeasures how much returns fluctuate; higher = more volatileLower = Smoother
Sortino RatioLike Sharpe but only penalizes downside volatility; more relevantHigher = Better
AUMTotal money managed; very high AUM in small cap can limit agilitySweet spot: ₹15K–60K Cr
Exit LoadPenalty for early redemption; standard is 1% if redeemed within 1 yearLower/Shorter = Better
Sortino RatioRisk-adjusted return focusing on downside volatility onlyHigher = Better
