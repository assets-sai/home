The Power of Compounding

Compounding is not just a formula — it's the single most powerful force in wealth creation. Understand how time, consistency, and patience can turn modest SIPs into crores.

"Compound interest is the eighth wonder of the world. He who understands it, earns it; he who doesn't, pays it." — Albert Einstein
🧪

What Exactly Is Compounding?

Compounding is when your returns generate their own returns. In Year 1, you earn returns on your investment. In Year 2, you earn returns on your investment plus the returns from Year 1. This snowball effect accelerates over time — and the longer you stay invested, the more dramatic the growth becomes. A ₹10,000/month SIP at 12% CAGR grows to ₹1 Crore in just 20 years — but your total investment is only ₹24 Lakhs. The remaining ₹76 Lakhs is pure compounding magic.

Golden Rules of Compounding

Follow these principles to maximize the compounding effect in your investment journey
⏰

Start Early, Even Small

A 25-year-old investing ₹5,000/month will beat a 35-year-old investing ₹15,000/month by retirement. Time is the single most powerful variable in the compounding equation.

🔄

Never Break the SIP

Missing SIPs during market crashes is the biggest wealth destroyer. Market dips are actually where SIPs buy more units at lower prices — this is rupee cost averaging working for you.

📈

Step Up Every Year

Increase your SIP by 10% annually with salary increments. A ₹10K SIP with 10% annual step-up grows to ₹2.6 Crore in 20 years vs ₹1 Crore without step-up.

🎯

Stay Equity-Heavy When Young

Equity delivers 12-15% CAGR over long periods. A 3% higher return doubles your final wealth over 25 years. Use equity-heavy allocation in your 20s and 30s.

🛑

Avoid Premature Withdrawals

Every ₹1 Lakh withdrawn at age 30 costs you ₹9.6 Lakhs at age 50 (at 12% CAGR). The opportunity cost of early withdrawal is staggering.

🧠

Let Math Win Over Emotion

Markets will crash, panic will spread, and headlines will scream. Ignore all of it. Compounding rewards patience, not timing. The best investors are the most boring ones.
